About Beacon

We live in Beacon, New York, a small city in the Hudson Valley, about 60 miles north of New York City.

How to Get Here

From New York City

Take the Metro North Railroad Hudson line to Poughkeepsie from Grand Central Station to Beacon Station. Check the schedules here. When you arrive at Beacon Station, you should be able to get a cab on the non-river side of the train station if you turn right as you walk up the stairs from the station. If you opt to take the train, there will be shuttles from our hotels, or you can take a cab to and from the University Settlement Camp

If there's no cab at the station when you arrive, call one of these cab companies:

  • Beacon Cab: (845) 625-6422
  • Clearport Limo & Taxi: (845) 283-7288
  • Empire Transportation: (845) 831-4444

From New Jersey (or driving from NYC)

Take the Palisades Parkway North to US-6 East. When the Palisades Parkway ends at the traffic circle, take the second exit onto US-202 East/US-6 East over the Bear Mountain Bridge. Immediately after the bridge, take a slight left onto NY-9D North. In about 15 miles, you'll reach the town of Beacon. As you enter town, 9D will be called Wolcott Avenue and the University Settlement Camp entrance will be on your right, at 724 Wolcott Avenue.

From Florida and anywhere else

Fly into John F. Kennedy (JFK), La Guardia (LGA), or Newark (EWR) and drive or take the Metro North from Grand Central to Beacon using the instructions above.

Or fly into Stewart International (SWF) and rent a car. Then take I-84 East to Exit 11, just after the Newburgh-Beacon Bridge. Turn right onto NY-9D South. In a few miles, the entrance to the University Settlement Camp will be on your left, at 724 Wolcott Avenue.

Main Street

Walk down Beacon's charming main street and check out some of our favorite haunts:

Food & Drink
  • Ella's Bellas, a cafe and gluten-free bakery at 420 Main Street
  • The Beacon Bagel, delicious bagels at 466 Main Street
  • Quinn's, a Japanese restaurant and jazz bar at 300 Main Street
  • Zora Dora, a popsicle shop at 201 Main Street
  • Kitchen Sink, a restaurant featuring local ingredients at 157 Main Street

Shopping
  • 13th Floor, a mid-century modern furniture shop at 456 Main Street
  • Blackbird Attic, a consignment shop at 442 Main Street

About Us

In May 2012, Jeremy started working at Etsy, and Lauren started two years after that. We first met on June 18, 2014, when we both went to visit the Etsy seller TwoFeathersNY in her studio with a group of other colleagues.

We didn't work on any of the same projects, so we didn't interact much after that, until the Etsy holiday party later that year, when our friend Lara not-so-subtly nudged us to dance together. We did, but it was pretty awkward.

In January of the following year, our colleage John announced a new project called Mixer to facilitate serendipitous one-on-one meetings between Etsy employees who might not otherwise have a chance to meet. We both signed up and were paired up for a meeting. Mixer's intention is to facilitate professional connections, of course, but after our mixer we started chatting a lot more, and eventually we went on our first date, to Brooklyn Heights Wine Bar.

This is the exact code that paired us up for our pre-first date:

func pickRandom(cur_person Person) (*Person, error) {
    list := rand.Perm(len(avail_pool))
    for i := range list {
        var random Person = avail_pool[i]
        if isValid(cur_person, random, true) {
            avail_pool = append(avail_pool[:i], avail_pool[i+1:]...)
            return &random, nil
        }
    }

    return nil, fmt.Errorf("couldn't find an available participant")
}

We're lucky that pickRandom returned random instead of nil!
