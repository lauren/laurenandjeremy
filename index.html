How to get here

See a map of all important locations for planning your transportation.

From New York City

Take the Metro North Railroad Hudson line towards Poughkeepsie from Grand Central Station to Beacon Station (not Beacon Falls!). Check the schedules here. When you arrive at Beacon Station, walk toward the rear of the train and go down the stairs, then turn left and go up another set of stairs. There should be cabs waiting to your right.

If there's no cab at the station when you arrive, call one of these cab companies:

  • Beacon Cab: (845) 625-6422
  • Clearport Limo & Taxi: (845) 283-7288
  • Empire Transportation: (845) 831-4444

From New Jersey (or driving from NYC)

Take the Palisades Parkway North to US-6 East. When the Palisades Parkway ends at the traffic circle, take the second exit onto US-202 East/US-6 East over the Bear Mountain Bridge. Immediately after the bridge, take a slight left at the stop sign onto NY-9D North. In about 15 miles, you'll reach the town of Beacon. As you enter town, 9D will be called Wolcott Avenue and the University Settlement Camp entrance will be on your right, at 724 Wolcott Avenue.

From Florida and anywhere else

We recommend that you fly into Stewart International (SWF) or Westchester County Airport (HPN) and rent a car. From SWF, take I-84 East to Exit 11, just after the Newburgh-Beacon Bridge. Turn right onto NY-9D South. In a few miles, the entrance to the University Settlement Camp will be on your left, at 724 Wolcott Avenue. From HPN, take I-684 North to exit 9W for I-84 West. Take I-84 exit 12 to NY-52 West. Turn left onto Old Glenham Road, left onto Maple Street, left onto Depuyster Avenue, left onto Howland Avenue, then left onto Wolcott Avenue. University Settlement camp will be on your left from this approach as well.

If there are no good flights to SWF or HPN, you can also fly into John F. Kennedy (JFK), La Guardia (LGA), or Newark (EWR), rent a car, and drive following the instructions under "From New Jersey" or take the Metro North from Grand Central to Beacon using the instructions under "From New York City."

About us

In May 2012, Jeremy started working at Etsy, and Lauren started two years after that. We first met on June 18, 2014, when we both went to visit the Etsy seller TwoFeathersNY in her studio with a group of other colleagues.

We didn't work on any of the same projects, so we didn't interact much after that, until the Etsy holiday party later that year, when our friend Lara not-so-subtly nudged us to dance together. We did, but it was pretty awkward.

In January of the following year, our colleage John announced a new project called Mixer to facilitate serendipitous one-on-one meetings between Etsy employees who might not otherwise have a chance to meet. We both signed up and were paired up for a meeting. Mixer's intention is to facilitate professional connections, of course, but after our mixer we started chatting a lot more, and eventually we went on our first date, to Brooklyn Heights Wine Bar.

How Jeremy got his coffee table

I needed a coffee table. I was also limited on space for a dining table and noticed that West Elm sold a storage coffee table that could double as a place to eat dinner. Perfect. I took some measurements, then ordered it with a 20% off coupon and had it delivered.

How Lauren got her coffee table

My ex and I were walking around Brooklyn and we found a pallet on the street that was in pretty good condition. We carried it two avenue blocks and up four flights of stairs to his apartment to use for a project. It sat there for a few months until I moved into an apartment that had room for a coffee table. We had made him a coffee table from a pallet before and I liked it, so we sanded the pallet in his apartment. Then we carried it down four flights of stairs, onto the subway for two stops, and up one flight of stairs to my apartment, where we stained it. While we were sealing it, he broke up with me. I finished sealing it myself and then bought large casters for it on Amazon to serve as the legs.
